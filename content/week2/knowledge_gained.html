Knowledge Gained

Chinese in Malaysia

Especially in Penang, I experienced firsthand the significant presence of the Chinese population in Malaysia. Many stores and restaurants had Cantonese-speaking staff, reflecting the cultural accommodation for the 23% of Malaysia's population that is Chinese.¹ This resonated deeply with me, as I am Chinese, allowing me to learn about the area's history while hearing the stories and journeys of others who share my heritage.

Ecotourism

Another notable observation was the potential of ecotourism and its role in biodiversity preservation. For example, Project Stampede, a collaboration between the Orang Asli and WWF, has successfully reduced tiger poaching by an impressive 98%, showcasing the impact of community-led conservation efforts.²
